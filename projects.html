Startup

Audemy

Over 70% of blind students are at least a grade level behind in school, so I created Audemy, an educational game startup for blind students.

Now, Audemy supports 1000+ blind students, works with 7 schools for the blind, and has grown from a team of 3 to 20+ volunteers.

Research

Heard-le

My friend couldn't play Wordle because she was blind, so I created an accessible, audio-based version of Wordle!

I developed this at SRL @ Texas A&M, and presented it at ACM International Conference for Multimodal Interaction in Paris, France.

Audio-Tactile Graph

Spacial mathematics concepts are difficult for many blind students to understand, as they are commonly conveyed visually. I built a coordinate plane system that uses an audio and tactile interface.

I built this at SRL @ Texas A&M and presented it at an ACM conference in San Jose, Costa Rica.

KatyYouthHacks

I wanted to build a hackathon for social impact, so I led KatyYouthHacks, a hackathon that tackles issues like sustainability and accessibility.

We’ve guided 1,300+ students from 27 countries to create 140+ projects, recruited 20+ judges, and raised $100,000+ in prizes.

alGIRLithm

I had a hard time finding other girls in the competitive programming sphere, so I lead alGIRLithm, a group that fosters community and real solutions for girls in tech.

Through the past three years, we have supported 300+ participants from 10+ countries and raised $12,000+ in sponsorships.
